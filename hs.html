The drawdown trick

Why daily loss catches you before max loss

The −5% daily limit and the −10% overall limit both apply in Phase 1, Phase 2 and the Funded stage. Here's how they interact — and why the −10% line only ever matters across two or more days.

Inside a single day
One trading session, falling fast
Stopped at −5%Daily limit triggers first — you can never reach −10% in one day.
Across 2+ days
Each day legal, the total is not
−10% breachedNo single day broke −5%, yet the running total crossed −10%.

The takeaway

The −5% daily loss resets each session, so the worst you can drop in one day is −5% — that's why it stops you before the −10% line is ever in reach. The −10% max loss only becomes the deciding rule when losses stack across two or more days (e.g. −5% then −5%). One bad day = the daily rule. A bad streak = the max-loss rule.

Two finish lines, working together

To pass / scale up the funded account you must hit both: reach the +10% target and log 3 profitable days. A profitable day is any day your total P/L closes at +0.50% or more — and it's only confirmed at the next midnight. Crucially: profitable days are only for scaling up. They have nothing to do with withdrawals.

Withdrawing before you pass

As long as you have not reached the +10% target and 3 profitable days, the account stays open and you can withdraw any time your profit is at least $150 — the same threshold for every account size, all the way up to the +10% target. Hitting profitable days does not lock or unlock anything here; they're purely for scaling.

When you fully pass

+10% and 3 profitable days

The instant both are met, this account is considered passed and closed. You don't withdraw from it — here's what happens instead.

🎯
01 · TRIGGER
Account passes & closes

You reach +10% and 3 profitable days. No withdrawal can be taken on this account — it's done.

📦
02 · TRANSFER
Profit moves over

Every dollar of profit is moved into your new funded account. Nothing is lost from the previous stage — it's just relocated.

⏳
03 · UNLOCK
14 days, then withdraw

Once 14 days have passed since account creation, withdrawal opens again — now from the new, larger account.
