What Are Futures?

A futures contract is a standardized agreement to buy or sell a specific asset (like the S&P 500 index, gold, or crude oil) at a predetermined price on a set date in the future. These contracts trade on regulated exchanges like the CME (Chicago Mercantile Exchange) and COMEX.

When you buy a stock, you own a piece of a company. With futures, you don't own anything — you're entering a contract based on the future price of an asset. Futures use leverage, meaning you only need a fraction of the full contract value (called "margin") to open a position. This makes them powerful but also risky if you don't manage your size properly.

Every futures contract has an expiration date. For example, ESH26 is the E-mini S&P 500 contract expiring in March 2026. The letter codes are: H = March, M = June, U = September, Z = December. When a contract nears expiration, you "roll" to the next one. On BlackArrow, you can use "continuous" tickers (like ESFUT) that automatically handle this for you.

A "tick" is the smallest price movement of a futures contract. Each tick has a fixed dollar value. For example, the E-mini S&P 500 (ES) moves in increments of 0.25 points, and each tick is worth $12.50. So if ES moves from 5000.00 to 5001.00, that's 4 ticks = $50 per contract. Understanding tick value is essential for calculating your risk per trade.

Futures trade on regulated exchanges with transparent pricing — there's no broker spread manipulation. You get real market depth, true volume data, and tighter execution. The cost structure is also transparent: you pay a fixed commission per contract, not a spread. This tends to be cheaper for active traders and allows for more precise risk management.

Start with Micro contracts (MES, MNQ, MYM, M2K). They're 1/10th the size of E-minis, so a 4-tick move on MES is only $5 instead of $50. This lets you practice real futures trading with controlled risk while you learn the platform and develop your strategy.

Quick Example

In Forex CFDs, if you go long 0.1 lots on EUR/USD and it moves 10 pips in your favor, you make $10. In futures, if you go long 1 MES (Micro S&P 500) contract and the price moves 4 ticks (1 point) in your favor, you make $5. The key difference is that in futures, the dollar value per tick is fixed — it doesn't change based on position size. You scale by adding more contracts.

Key Rules to Know

Breaking these rules will fail your evaluation. Read them carefully and plan your trading around them.

⚠️ Maximum Drawdown: 3%

Your account cannot lose more than 3% from its highest balance. This is the hard limit — if you hit it, the evaluation ends. Calculate your risk per trade carefully.

📊 Consistency Rule: 30%

No single trading day can account for more than 30% of your total profits. This prevents "all or nothing" gambling. Trade consistently across multiple days.

🎯 Profit Target: 6%

Reach 6% profit on your account balance to pass the evaluation. Take your time — there's no time limit. Consistency and risk management matter more than speed.

Futures Glossary

Quick reference for terminology you'll encounter.

Tick
The smallest price increment a contract can move. Example: ES moves in 0.25 increments.
Tick Value
The dollar amount you gain or lose per tick, per contract. ES = $12.50/tick, MES = $1.25/tick.
Point
Usually equals 4 ticks on index futures. A 1-point move on ES = 4 × $12.50 = $50.
Contract
One unit of a futures instrument. You buy/sell in whole contracts (1, 2, 3…), not fractional lots.
Round Turn
A complete trade — opening and closing a position. Commission is quoted per round turn.
Roll / Rollover
Moving your position from an expiring contract to the next active one.
CME
Chicago Mercantile Exchange — the main exchange where most futures are traded.
Margin
The deposit required to hold a futures position. Not a loan — it's collateral.
Drawdown
The decline from your account's highest point. At The5ers, max allowed is 3%.
OCO Order
One-Cancels-Other — a bracket order where your stop-loss and take-profit are linked.
Continuous Ticker
A symbol (like ESFUT) that automatically rolls to the next contract when the current one expires.

🧠 Final Tips for Your First Week

Don't rush. Spend at least a few days on the simulator getting comfortable with BlackArrow before placing real trades on your evaluation.

Trade small. Start with 1 Micro contract. You can always scale up once you're consistent.

Know your tick value. Before every trade, know exactly how much each tick costs you. This is your foundation for risk management.

Always use a stop-loss. Futures move fast. An unprotected position can blow through your 3% drawdown limit in minutes on a volatile day.

Watch the live streams. The5ers Futures YouTube channel has regular live trading and educational content. Real-time learning is invaluable.
