01
The Rule Explained

Your $25,000 account is protected by an EOD Trailing Drawdown of 3%. This means your loss floor trails upward every time you close a trading day at a new equity high — but never moves down. The floor is always 3% below your highest recorded EOD closing balance. If your live account balance touches this floor at any moment — intraday or at close — the account is immediately breached and closed.

03
Step-by-Step Mechanics
1
Day 0 — Account Opened
Floor Is Set
Your initial loss floor is placed 3% below $25,000. That gives you exactly $750 of breathing room from the very first trade.
$25,000 × 0.97 = $24,250 floor
2
During the Session
Live P&L Watched
Your live balance is monitored every tick. The floor does not move intraday, but a breach can happen at any moment. If balance hits $24,250 (or current floor), it's over immediately.
3
Market Close (EOD)
Watermark Compared
At close, your EOD balance is compared to the previous EOD high watermark. Only a new EOD high causes the floor to trail upward.
New EOD High × 0.97 = New Floor
4
No New High
Floor Stays Fixed
A down day compresses your buffer but cannot lower the floor. The trail is strictly one-directional. Once locked in, a level is permanent until a new high is made.
5
Profit Lock-In
Floor Reaches $25K
Once your EOD high exceeds ~$25,773, the trailing floor crosses your starting balance. On most platforms it then locks permanently at $25,000 — your funded capital is fully protected.
EOD High ≥ $25,773 → Floor = $25,000
6
Breach Event
Account Closed
Live balance drops to or below current floor → immediate liquidation of all positions. Account is closed. You must re-purchase an evaluation or restart the qualification process.

06
Critical Rules to Know
📅
EOD Only — Intraday Highs Don't Trail
An intraday spike to $27,000 that closes at $25,600 only updates the floor from $25,600 × 0.97 — not $27,000. Intraday peaks are irrelevant to the trail calculation.
⚡
Intraday Breach Is Instant — No Grace Period
Even with a $750 buffer, a single bad trade can trigger a breach. If live P&L drops to the current floor during any trading session, the account is closed immediately with no recovery window.
↑
Floor Never Goes Down — Locks In Gains
Once you've pushed the floor up through consistent EOD highs, that protection is permanent. A losing day only reduces your buffer — it cannot lower an already-established floor.
🔒
Lock-In Threshold — ~$25,773 EOD High
On a $25,000 account, once your EOD high watermark reaches approximately $25,773, the trailing floor crosses your starting balance. Most firms then lock the floor at $25,000 permanently — your funded capital is safe forever.
⚠️
CRITICAL — $750 IS THIN: On a $25,000 account, your entire buffer is just $750. One ES contract can move $50/tick — a 15-tick adverse move wipes the buffer. Always ensure your worst-case per-trade risk is less than 25% of the current buffer ($187.50 or less). Never add to losing positions.
