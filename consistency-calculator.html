How the 40% rule works

The consistency rule stops a trader from passing on one lucky trade. To clear it, profit must be spread out — no single trade can carry more than 40% of everything you've made. Because it measures each trade (not the day's net), it is stricter than a day-based rule: one fill can't dominate the account.

Best Trade ÷ Total Net Profit  <  40%   ⟶   Total > Best Trade × 2.5

▲ Winning trades

A winner grows your total — good for the target. But if one trade is far bigger than the rest, its share climbs toward and past the 40% cap. The fix is more winners of similar size, not one monster trade. Every additional winning trade raises the total, which lowers your best trade's percentage and pulls you back into compliance.

▼ Losing trades

A loser shrinks your total net profit. Since your best trade is divided by a smaller total, its percentage goes up — so a loss makes consistency worse even though your best trade never changed. Recovering with several modest winners, rather than one large one, restores both the total and the ratio.
