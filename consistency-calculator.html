📖 How the 30% Consistency Rule Works

The 30% consistency rule ensures traders demonstrate consistent performance rather than relying on one oversized winning trade. This rule applies to both evaluation and funded accounts.

✓ The Rule: No single trade may account for more than 30% of your total profits.

What is a "Soft Breach"?
A soft breach occurs when a single trade exceeds 30% of your current total profit. Unlike a hard breach, this does NOT immediately fail your account. Instead, you simply need to continue trading and increase your total profits until that oversized trade drops below the 30% threshold.

Example: You make a $1,200 trade
• $1,200 ÷ 0.30 = $4,000 total profit required
• Current total: $2,000 → Soft breach (60%)
• Once total hits $4,000 → $1,200 = 30% → Compliant

Key Takeaway: You can pass evaluation in one day, but you must maintain consistency. If you soft breach, keep trading to dilute that large trade. There's no time penalty — just keep building total profit.
