7-Step Execution Flow
1
Mark Up the Session Range 04:00–11:00 GMT+1
Identify the High and Low formed during the 4–11 AM GMT+1 session. This range becomes your entire framework — liquidity sits at both extremes.
2
Wait for a Liquidity Sweep
Gold must wick the High or Low of the 4–11 AM range. This sweep takes out resting stop-losses and traps breakout traders, creating the fuel for reversal.
3
Order Block Takeout → Full Reversal
An Order Block must be taken out with price showing a full reversal. This is your structural confirmation — smart money has engaged and flipped direction.
4
Identify the Imbalance (FVG)
An imbalance / Fair Value Gap must exist. The preferred imbalance is on the candle that took out the Order Block — this is where price is most likely to return for a retest.
5
Reversal Must Happen in Kill Zone 13:00–16:00 GMT+1
The Order Block reversal must occur between 13:00–16:00 GMT+1. This is the New York kill zone overlap — maximum volume and highest probability for institutional reversals.
6
Set Limit Order Inside the Imbalance
Place your limit order inside the imbalance (FVG). The actual trigger / fill can happen outside the 13:00–16:00 window — you're pre-positioned for the retest. Stop-loss below the sweep low.
7
Target: Opposite Side of the Range 💰 Take Profit
Target the High or Low of the 4–11 AM range — whichever is the opposite end from the sweep. If Low was swept → target the High. If High was swept → target the Low.
Edge Notes
⏱
Time is a Filter
If the reversal doesn't form inside 13:00–16:00, there's no trade. The session overlap is a non-negotiable confluence.
◧
Imbalance = Entry Zone
The FVG on the OB-breaking candle is the highest-probability re-entry. Price fills the gap before continuing.
⛨
Sweep = Fuel
No wick beyond the range = no trade. The liquidity grab is what powers the reversal move back through the range.
⟷
Works Both Directions
System is symmetrical. Sweep the high → short to the low. Sweep the low → long to the high. Same rules, mirrored.
